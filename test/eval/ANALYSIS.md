## Reading these numbers

### What the engine reliably catches (deterministic)

- **Net-contents semantic mismatches** (5% tolerance) ÔÇö declared `750 mL` vs extracted `500 mL` Ôćĺ `flag`
- **Brand-vs-different-brand** ÔÇö declared `Half Acre` vs extracted `NONEXISTENT MISMATCH BRAND XYZ` Ôćĺ `flag`
- **ABV mismatches** (>0.1% delta) ÔÇö both high and low Ôćĺ `flag`
- **Unit swaps** ÔÇö declared `100 mL` vs extracted `750 mL` Ôćĺ `flag`
- **Verbatim Government Warning paraphrase** when the model transcribes accurately

### What the engine routes to human review (with explicit reasons)

- Extractions where the model's confidence falls below 0.60 Ôćĺ demoted from match to likely
- Multi-image COLAs where ABV / net contents / bottler is on a panel not provided Ôćĺ `likely` with note
- Unmeasurable font size (every real image, in practice) Ôćĺ `fontSizeOk=False` with "please confirm manually" + 16.22 threshold cited
- Subtle warning spelling/punctuation errors where OCR normalization papers over them (8/20 TTB-noted findings) ÔÇö covered in `docs/COVERAGE.md` ┬žC

### Why "73% needs-review" on truly-approved labels isn't a failure

The needs-review label is the engine's **conservative default** when:
- COLA form `BRAND_NAME` Ôëá label's prominent text (which is often `PRODUCT_NAME` / fanciful SKU)
- COLA form omits the bottler line Ôćĺ field-level "likely with note"
- Dataset's `OCR_VOLUME` captured a different unit than the label printed
- Container-size mismatches when the COLA covers multiple keg sizes

Per `docs/COVERAGE.md`: "Nothing is silently auto-passed." The engine prefers
to route uncertain cases to the agent rather than risk a false auto-pass.

## Engine improvements that landed during eval iteration

Every refinement is locked in by a unit test (`backend/tests/`).

1. **Case-insensitive verbatim warning** ÔÇö 16.21 fixes wording, 16.22 fixes only the heading case (all-caps body printed verbatim is compliant). Warning false-flag fell from 87% Ôćĺ 27%.
2. **OCR-noise punctuation tightening** ÔÇö `WARNING :` Ôćĺ `WARNING:`, `( 1 )` Ôćĺ `(1)`.
3. **`casingBoldOk` drops the `heading_bold` gate** ÔÇö model can't reliably detect bold from a single image; gates on `casing_all_caps and not body_bold`. Heading bold is flagged for visual confirmation by the agent.
4. **Country of origin prefix strip** ÔÇö strips `Product of / Imported from / Made in / Bottled in / Distilled in`, case-insensitive.
5. **Net contents semantic match** ÔÇö parse both sides to mL; within 5% = match. Handles `1 PINT (16 FL OZ)`, `12 L 40.5 FL OZ 2.53 PINT`.
6. **Alcohol content semantic match** ÔÇö numeric % within 0.1.
7. **Umbrella class types** ÔÇö 40+ COLA codes (`beer`, `ale`, `table red wine`, `whisky specialties`, etc.) match any printed sub-designation.
8. **Missing extracted on a non-warning field** Ôćĺ `likely` (multi-image reality) instead of `flag`.
9. **Flavored-malt class-type auto-detects added flavors** ÔÇö seltzers correctly require ABV per 7.63.
10. **Empty declared on required field** Ôćĺ `match` with transparent note (COLA form omissions, e.g. bottler).
11. **Low-confidence promotion** ÔÇö extractor confidence < 0.60 demotes `match` Ôćĺ `likely` so a confident-but-wrong AI read can't auto-pass.
12. **Tighter brand containment** ÔÇö a short declared brand inside long unrelated extracted text is `flag`, not `likely` (Ôëą60% length-ratio required for the containment rule).

## Dataset construction (`test/eval/build_dataset.py`)

Three slices, all from the COLA Cloud free sample (CC0):

1. **`clean_approved`** (60 rows) ÔÇö stratified across 21 product categories; warning visible in OCR; keg-size ambiguity excluded; OCR-derived ABV + net contents from the dataset.
2. **`ttb_gws_finding`** (20 rows) ÔÇö real approved COLAs where `APPROVAL_QUALIFICATIONS` contains spelling/punctuation findings TTB recorded. The gold-standard "should flag" set.
3. **`mutation`** (10 rows) ÔÇö declared application data flipped on copies of clean approved labels. 7 mutation kinds: `shrink_net_contents` ┬Ě `swap_net_contents_unit` ┬Ě `brand_mismatch` ┬Ě `abv_mismatch_high` ┬Ě `abv_mismatch_low` ┬Ě `country_origin_mismatch` ┬Ě `class_type_mismatch`. Each kind is only applied to a row whose beverage type makes the violation reachable (no ABV mutations on plain beer ÔÇö ┬ž7.63 makes ABV optional).

The `cola_sample.csv` is committed (small, reproducible, ~90 rows). The
bulk source CSVs and downloaded images are gitignored ÔÇö run
`make build-eval-data` to populate locally.

## Why we don't have rejected-status labels from the TTB Public COLA Registry

The data.gov "TTB Public COLA Registry Search and Download" resource is a
link to the **interactive search UI** at `ttbonline.gov/colasonline/`,
not a bulk-downloadable CSV. The Advanced Search supports filtering by
Status (approved / expired / surrendered / revoked) but results return
as paginated HTML ÔÇö no public bulk CSV endpoint exists.

The COLA Cloud free sample is approved-only by definition (the COLA
Registry only publishes approved labels ÔÇö rejected applications never
enter the public registry).

That's why the harness uses two fallbacks for "should-flag" cases:

- **TTB-noted findings on approved labels** (20 records) ÔÇö real labels
  where TTB explicitly recorded a finding in the public record.
- **Mutations of approved labels** (10 records) ÔÇö engineered to force
  specific violation types.

Both serve as "must flag" benchmarks; the `APPROVAL_QUALIFICATIONS`
field is, in practice, TTB's own documentation of issues they caught.
